About the Project

This project analyzes job offers in the data field to uncover typical patterns across companies, skills, and regions. It combines descriptive statistics, text mining, and clustering methods to reveal how jobs naturally group together.

üé• Watch the Explanation Video

Objective

The goal is to identify structural trends and natural groupings among job offers using derived textual data. We apply clustering algorithms to highlight key job profiles such as ‚ÄúBusiness & BI‚Äù vs. ‚ÄúEngineering & AI‚Äù.

Motivation

The data job market is vast and constantly evolving. Titles and skills vary across companies and regions. This project uses a mix of semantic and statistical methods to make these dynamics visible and interpretable ‚Äî mapping how the field is structured in practice.

Main Tasks

  • Data cleaning and normalization of job descriptions
  • Automatic extraction of skills, experience, and work mode
  • Descriptive analysis by company, country, and role
  • Clustering and visualization using PCA / t-SNE
  • Qualitative interpretation of the resulting clusters

Expected Results

  • An enriched dataset with derived columns (skills, sector...)
  • Visualizations: distributions, maps, word clouds
  • Clusters of similar job offers
  • Interpretation of trends across roles and regions

Key Insights

Data Cleaning

Removal of invalid rows and normalization of text fields to ensure high-quality data for further processing.

Skill Extraction

Automatic detection of technologies such as Python, SQL, Tableau, or PowerBI from job descriptions.

Clustering

Grouping similar job offers together based on their text embeddings to reveal hidden structures in the job market.

MVP & Resources

The MVP demonstrates feasibility by clustering processed job offers based on description similarity. Tools include Python (pandas, scikit-learn, spaCy), and D3.js for visualization.
