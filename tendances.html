Decoded: What this map really tells us

This visualization is more than just colored dots; it is a semantic territory map of thousands of job descriptions. By using dimensionality reduction (SVD), we've translated textual similarities into spatial distances.

1. A Shared Technical Core

Observation: The Data Product, AI Strategy, and ML Engineering clusters are distinct but situated very close to each other in the center-left region.

Interpretation: While these roles have different end-goals (strategy vs. building vs. product insights), they rely on the **same dense technical vocabulary** in job descriptions (e.g., Python, Cloud, advanced algorithms). They form the "heavy technical backbone" of the industry.

2. The Great "Business Intelligence" Divide

Observation: While some Green dots (BI) are near the technical core, a significant sub-group is completely isolated on the far right.

Interpretation: "Business Intelligence" is a fractured term. It encompasses two very different jobs today: deeply technical BI Analysts (integrated with engineering) and traditional Reporting Specialists (far removed from code).

3. The Vertical Axis: Scope vs. Execution

Observation: Generalist profiles float at the top, while Junior roles cluster at the very bottom.

Interpretation: This vertical separation reveals a hierarchy of complexity. Entry-level jobs use a fundamentally different vocabulary—focused on basic task execution—compared to the highly versatile, strategic vocabulary of senior generalists.

Key Takeaway

The data job market is no longer a flat list of titles. It has evolved into a highly integrated technical ecosystem at its center, with distinct, specialized satellites orbiting it.
